Frequently Asked Questions

SHOULD I SIG IN? HOW LONG IS THIS GOING TO TAKE?

If you’re looking for a long-term relationship, you probably should create an account

Even if you do immediately find the person of your dreams, it’ll take a few months of dating before you know whether you’re officially out of the dating pool.

That said, if you know exactly what you’re looking for and you have a strategy, it may only take a few weeks. Once I had my own strategy in place, the next date I went on turned out to be my last one ever.

WILL ANYONE ACTUALLY READ MY PROFILE, OR ARE THEY JUST LOOKING AT MY PHOTOS? In part because of how dating sites are designed, most of us see photos first, and that’s when we determine whether to read through the rest of a profile. It has to do more with neuroscience than superficiality.

We’re drawn to photos over text, because we can process that visual information and make inferences more quickly.

Are they half-hearted selfies? Or do you look absolutely stunning – showing a little skin, wearing fresh makeup, looking happy?

I DON’T WANT ANYONE TO KNOW WHO I AM IN REAL LIFE. WHAT HAPPENS IF I DECIDE NOT TO INCLUDE A PHOTO?

Nothing happens, you can still searching through profiles and making your choices of match, but the possibilities of not being selected increase. Chances are extremely good that few people will click through your profile. If they do send you a message, a photo is likely to be the first thing they ask for.

I LIVE IN A VERY SMALL TOWN WITH SLIM PICKINGS. WILL ONLINE DATING HELP ME?

If you’re willing to expand your reach to the maximum number of miles allowed, or if you’re able to drive to the next town over, then yes. But you need to be explicit and honest about where you live early on – and you need to be willing to put in the effort to drive out to see the people you’re meeting.

I LIVE IN A MASSIVE CITY WITH MILLIONS OF POSSIBILITIES – WHY CAN’T I FIND ANYONE GOOD ONLINE?

Enough to create a curiosity gap.

Use the same approach when writing your profile.

Create a sense of mystery and excitement and give people a concrete reason to contact you.

WHY ISN’T ANYONE CONTACTING ME? WHAT DID I DO WRONG?

There are many variables, so try to evaluate each one. Are you using the best possible photos? Did you write an extremely long profile? Or one that’s too short? Did you try to use sarcasm? Maybe it’s coming across as bitter rather than funny.

It’s entirely possible, though, that you’ve done nothing wrong at all and that you have a very good profile. Going in to refresh your profile once a day could potentially help, depending on the dating site you’re using. Some reward more active users with better placement (especially if they filter by last log in or update).
